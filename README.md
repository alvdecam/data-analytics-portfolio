## About

I trained as a mechanical and electrical engineer at École Centrale de Lyon and Universidad de Sevilla, where I ranked first nationally in Mechanical Engineering (SEDEA Award, 2020). My early research work — at CNRS, AICIA and Bosch — was already heavily quantitative: finite element modelling in MATLAB, sensor validation testing, experimental data processing for ABB.

For the last four years I have worked as a Functional Consultant at **Manhattan Associates**, leading Order Management System implementations for international retail brands including **Christian Louboutin, Nespresso, L'Occitane and SuitSupply**. The work sits at the intersection of operations and data: building IBM Cognos dashboards over supply-chain and order data, writing Python and Postman automation that removed manual operational workflows, and monitoring system KPIs through Kibana log analysis. Translating messy operational data into something a business leader can act on has been the through-line of my job, even before I called it "data analytics."

I am now formalising this transition through an **M.Sc. in Data Science at FernUniversität in Hagen** (started 2026) and the **Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate**. This portfolio is where I apply what I learn to end-to-end projects: framing a business question, auditing the data, cleaning it, exploring it, and communicating findings clearly.

## Projects

### [Employee Satisfaction Analysis — Fiktiva](./employee-satisfaction-fiktiva)
End-to-end analysis of a synthetic HR dataset (700 employees). Covers data auditing, cleaning, plausibility checks and focused exploratory analysis on seven business questions. Main finding: Marketing satisfaction is 16 points below Sales, and recent-hire satisfaction has been declining since 2020.
*Tools: Python, pandas, matplotlib, Jupyter.*
